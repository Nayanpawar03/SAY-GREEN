CHEMICAL DEGRADATION

Chemical recycling involves breaking down bioplastics into their molecular components through processes like depolymerization or pyrolysis. These methods convert bioplastics into monomers or other valuable chemicals that can be used to produce new plastics or other materials.

MECHANICAL DEGRADATION

Certain types of bioplastics, such as PLA (polylactic acid), can be mechanically recycled by melting and reshaping them into new products. However, this process may degrade the material's quality over time, limiting its recyclability.

PHOTODEGRADATION

Certain bioplastics can degrade when exposed to sunlight or ultraviolet (UV) radiation, which breaks down the polymer chains into smaller fragments. However, this method may not be suitable for all bioplastics and requires specific environmental conditions to occur effectively.
