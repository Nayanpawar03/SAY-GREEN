The Issue

Plastic pollution is an escalating environmental crisis with far-reaching consequences for ecosystems and human well-being.Around 275 million tonnes of plastic waste is generated each year around the world; between 4.8 million and 12.7 million tonnes is dumped into the sea. About 60% of the plastic waste in the ocean comes from the top five countries: China, Indonesia, the Philippines, Thailand and Vietnam.

Harm to Marine Life

In marine ecosystems, plastic debris accumulates in vast ocean gyres, forming colossal garbage patches that endanger marine life through ingestion and entanglement. Marine animals mistake plastic fragments for food, leading to internal injuries, digestive blockages, and, ultimately, death. Additionally, discarded fishing gear, known as ghost nets, continues to trap marine animals indiscriminately, exacerbating the problem.

Land Pollution

On land, plastic waste mars landscapes, clogs waterways, and infiltrates soil, posing hazards to terrestrial wildlife and ecosystems. Microplastics, resulting from the breakdown of larger plastic items, further exacerbate the issue, permeating soil and water sources and infiltrating the food chain, with potential repercussions for human health.

Necessary Approch

Addressing the plastic pollution crisis demands a multifaceted approach encompassing policy reform, innovation in waste management and recycling technologies, consumer education, and corporate responsibility. Strategies such as phasing out single-use plastics, promoting circular economy models, and investing in sustainable alternatives offer promising avenues for mitigating plastic pollution and safeguarding the health of ecosystems and future generations.
